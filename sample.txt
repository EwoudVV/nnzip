The morning rain pattered against the windows of the small cottage at the edge of the village. Margaret stirred her tea slowly, watching the steam rise and curl in the cool air. The fire in the hearth had burned low overnight, leaving only glowing embers that pulsed weakly with each draft from the chimney. She would need to fetch more wood from the shed before the day turned colder, but for now she savored the quiet stillness of the hour before anyone else in the house would wake. Outside, the world was wrapped in a soft gray mist that softened every edge and muted every sound. A pair of crows called to each other from somewhere in the orchard, their voices distant and lonely. Margaret reached for the letter on the table beside her, the one that had arrived yesterday and which she had read three times already without quite believing what it said.
